## Hardware

### Desktop

- **Custom Desktop PC:**
  - **CPU**: [AMD Ryzen 5 9600X](https://www.amd.com/en/products/processors/desktops/ryzen/9000-series/amd-ryzen-5-9600x.html) - I find this CPU to be the best balance of performance and price. It uses the AM5 socket so I can upgrade it later.
  - **Motherboard**: [MSI MAG B850M Mortar WiFi](https://www.msi.com/Motherboard/MAG-B850M-MORTAR-WIFI/Specification) - A little more expensive than other options, but it has WiFi 7 support which I needed since I don't have an Ethernet port on the wall.
  - **RAM**: 32GB DDR5 (6000 MHz CL30) - I bought this RAM just before the market crashed. I could have gone with 16GB, but I got a good deal on this one.
  - **Storage**: [2TB NVMe SSD](https://www.samsung.com/us/memory-storage/nvme-ssd/990-pro-pcie-4-0-nvme-ssd-1tb-sku-mz-v9p2t0b-am) - 2TB gives me enough space to comfortably run a dual boot without worrying about storage. Fast and reliable, not much else to say about it.
  - **GPU**: [Sapphire Nitro+ Radeon RX 9060 XT](https://www.sapphiretech.com/en/consumer/nitro-radeon-rx-9060-xt-16g-gddr6) - I went with AMD for better Linux compatibility. I picked the Nitro+ version because it was on sale and I really like the design.
  - **Case**: [Lian Li Dan A3](https://lian-li.com/product/a3-matx) - I wanted a small form factor case with good airflow. It is great, though it was a bit tricky for a first build due to the number of possible configurations.
  - **CPU Cooler**: [Noctua NH-D15S](https://www.noctua.at/en/products/nh-d15s-chromax-black#hero) - Keeps my CPU cool and quiet. I chose the NH-D15S specifically because it has only one fan, since two fans would have taken too much space in my case.
  - **Power Supply**: [Corsair SFX 850W](https://www.corsair.com/fr/en/p/psu/cp-9020256-eu/sf-series-sf850-fully-modular-80-plus-platinum-sfx-power-supply-eu-cp-9020256-eu) - Very happy with this PSU. The fan never spins, and the small form factor along with the braided cables makes cable management much easier.
- **Keyboard:** [Nuphy Air75 V3](https://nuphy.com/products/nuphy-air75-v3) - A great low-profile keyboard with wired, wireless, and Bluetooth support. I picked the silent switches so I can use it comfortably in an open-space at work.
- **Monitor:** [Lenovo Legion R27qe](https://psref.lenovo.com/Detail/Lenovo_Legion_R27qe?M=67C5GAC1US) - I got it for under $150 which was a great deal. Nothing particularly impressive about it, but it does the job well for the price.

### Music

- **MIDI Keyboard:** [Swissonic EasyKey 49](https://www.thomannmusic.com/swissonic_easykey_49.htm) - This is my first instrument. Since I am self-teaching piano, I didn't want to spend too much before knowing if I would stick with it. I really recommend it though. The build quality is solid and the 49-key format is perfect if you just want to play for fun without taking up too much space.
